# Simple Interest Calculator

## Purpose
The Simple Interest Calculator is a lightweight Bash script designed to quickly calculate the simple interest accrued on a principal amount over a specified time period at a given annual interest rate.

## How It Works
The script prompts the user to input three key values:
1. **Principal Amount ($P$)**: The initial amount of money borrowed or invested.
2. **Rate of Interest ($R$)**: The annual interest rate (as a percentage).
3. **Time Period ($T$)**: The duration for which the money is borrowed or invested (in years).

It then computes the total simple interest and displays the resulting amount to the terminal.

## Formula
Simple Interest ($SI$) is calculated using the standard formula:

$$SI = \frac{P \times R \times T}{100}$$

Where:
* **$P$** = Principal amount
* **$R$** = Annual interest rate (percentage)
* **$T$** = Time period in years

## Example
If you invest **$1,000** at an annual interest rate of **5%** for **3 years**:

* **$P$** = 1000
* **$R$** = 5
* **$T$** = 3

$$SI = \frac{1000 \times 5 \times 3}{100} = 150$$

**Output:** The simple interest accrued is **$150**. Total amount after 3 years is **$1,150**.
